Using distractors in multiple choice mode

Distractors are used in multiple choice mode.

 

Multiple choice questions are a form of assessment in which you are asked to select one or more of the choices from a list. They consist of a stem and a set of options.

 

  • The stem is the beginning part of the item that presents the item as a problem to be solved, a question asked, or an incomplete statement to be completed, as well as any other relevant information.

  • The options are the possible answers that the examinee can choose from, with the correct answer(s) called the key(s) and the incorrect answers called distractors.

 

Defining wrong answers individually instead of using random distractors from the pool of answers, enhances your learning experience.

 

To create a card with distractors

  1. Within the Edit menu, click either Add Card or Maintain Cards.

  2. In the Answer field, type the correct answer and as many incorrect answers as you like in the same way as synonyms and separate them by pressing Enter or by clicking and selecting the next empty row.

  3. Convert any entry in this list to a distractor by right-clicking on it and selecting Distractor.

  4. Click Add card or Save Changes.
